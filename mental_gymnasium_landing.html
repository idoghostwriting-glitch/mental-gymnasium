01 — the zine

Twenty A5 pages you can actually hold

Four chapters, named only by their first line. A complete passage in four movements: spiral → breath → turn → repair.

fictocriticism

Thinks and bleeds

Existential inquiry — time, value, the “separated self” — carried in stream-of-consciousness. It reasons and it confesses.

an object

Not content — a thing

Hand-numbered, printed once, in a cold Prussian-and-pine palette. In an infinite scroll, this is finite and ownable.

a passage

A way through

It converses with ideas like the body keeps the score — moving past the bottom toward self-acceptance and the repair you needed.

02 — who made it

A Thai writer, working in English, from nights genuinely survived

I wrote this not to drown in the dark, but to find the way out — and to show that the way out can be written down.

I’m Neo_SC. Mental Gymnasium began as field notes from a mind that wouldn’t sleep — a passage through addiction, perfectionism, and the long work of repair. I write in English, my second language, because the distance lets me look straight at things I couldn’t in any other voice.

This is Issue 1 of an ongoing practice. The gym is open; issues arrive when they’re ready — no treadmill. If the work stirs something in you, I’d like you to stay a little longer.
